## DPC (DIRECT PRIMARY CARE) MEMBERSHIP

### What Is DPC?
A membership-based primary care model with a flat monthly fee. No insurance claims, no copays, no surprise bills. Patients pay a predictable monthly rate and get unlimited access to Dr. Reve.

### Key Benefits
- **Unlimited visits** — See Dr. Reve as often as needed
- **Same-day appointments** — Priority scheduling
- **Direct access** — Phone, text, and video access to Dr. Reve
- **30–60 minute appointments** — Real time with your doctor
- **No copays** — The monthly fee covers visits
- **Transparent pricing** — No hidden costs or surprise bills
- **Wholesale medications** — Medications dispensed at cost
- **HSA-eligible** — Starting 2026, membership fees can be paid with Health Savings Account funds

### Who Is DPC For?
- Uninsured patients seeking affordable primary care
- Patients with high-deductible health plans
- Anyone who values direct, unhurried access to their physician
- Patients frustrated with the traditional insurance model

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### Q: Is Faithful Care accepting new patients?
A: Yes! Faithful Care Medical Services welcomes new patients. Call (239) 423-0205 or WhatsApp +1 (786) 817-1932 to schedule your first visit.

### Q: Do I need a referral to be seen?
A: No referral is needed for primary care visits. Simply call to schedule an appointment.

### Q: How long are appointments?
A: Appointments are typically 30–60 minutes. Dr. Reve takes the time to listen and address all of your concerns.

### Q: Can I get a same-day appointment?
A: Yes. Same-day appointments are one of the practice's core commitments. Call in the morning, and Dr. Reve can often see you that same day.

### Q: Does Faithful Care offer telemedicine/virtual visits?
A: DPC members have access to phone, text, and video consultations with Dr. Reve. Call the office for details on virtual visit availability.

### Q: What is the difference between palliative care and hospice?
A: Palliative care is an extra layer of support available at any stage of a serious illness. It works alongside your regular treatment to manage symptoms and improve quality of life. Hospice is specifically for end-of-life care. Faithful Care provides palliative care, not hospice, though they can coordinate hospice services when appropriate.

### Q: Do you speak Spanish?
A: The practice is bilingual. Patients can communicate in English or Spanish.

### Q: What should I bring to my first appointment?
A: Bring a valid photo ID, your insurance card (if applicable), a list of current medications, and any recent medical records or test results you have.

### Q: How much does DPC membership cost?
A: For specific pricing, please call (239) 423-0205 or WhatsApp +1 (786) 817-1932. The team will explain the membership options and help you find the right fit.

### Q: Can I use my insurance AND be a DPC member?
A: DPC membership covers primary care visits directly. Your insurance can still be used for specialist referrals, lab work, imaging, and hospital services. Call the office to discuss how DPC and insurance can work together for your situation.
